PUNJAB DHOL

THE FESTIVAL OF FIRE

LOHRI

Welcoming the Sun and the Harvest

The Significance

Lohri marks the end of the peak of winter and is a traditional welcome of longer days and the sun's journey to the northern hemisphere. It is a day of Thanksgiving for the farmers of Punjab.

The bonfire represents the Sun God, and offerings of sesame seeds (til), jaggery (gur), and peanuts are made to the fire to seek abundance and prosperity.

The Bonfire

The heart of Lohri. People circle the fire, singing folk songs and dancing the Bhangra.

Dulha Bhatti

Folk songs are sung in praise of Dulha Bhatti, a legendary hero who rescued girls from slavery.

Winter Treats

Sarson da Saag, Makki di Roti, and sweets made of peanuts and rewari are the soul of the feast.
